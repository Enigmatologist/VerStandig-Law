Named a 2009 and 2010 Superlawyer Rising Star, Jordan A. Handrich has a strong background in the area of construction defect and mold/water intrusion litigation. She has represented clients consisting of homeowner associations, home owners, and companies who have construction defect problems ranging from defective stucco to bad roof systems. After attending the University of Minnesota Law School (J.D., 2001), Jordan was admitted to the Minnesota Bar in 2001. She joined Harper & Peterson in 2004 as a part-time project attorney and became a permanent addition to the firm in May, 2005. She has recently accepted a position with VerStandig Law Firm and is excited to work in and with the gaming community as Handrich herself is a very accomplished poker player.

Background

Mr. VerStandig came up through one of Maryland's largest and preeminent law firms, learning his way around the courtroom and boardroom alike under the tutelage of some of the Mid-Atlantic’s top attorneys. More than half a decade — and hundreds of cases — later, he departed to form his own practice, taking with him an eclectic array of clients and focusing intently on their needs in a more personal and flexible legal setting. Prior to entering private pracice, Mr. VerStandig studied under the Hon. John K. Olson on the United States Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of Florida. He then served as law clerk to the Hon. Steven G. Salant on the Circuit Court for Montgomery County, Maryland, managing a wide array of civil matters and gaining appreciable experience in the field of domestic relations law.

Community Involvement

Jordan has been heavily involved in the legal community.
